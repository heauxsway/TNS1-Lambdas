Who We Are

More Than a Fraternity

Lambda Theta Phi Latin Fraternity, Inc. is the first and largest Latin fraternity in the United States. Founded on the principle of uplifting the Latino community, we cultivate leaders who serve with integrity, excel academically, and build bonds that last a lifetime.

Tennessee Sector 1 represents four active chapters across the state — united in scholarship, cultural pride, and service to our communities from Nashville to Chattanooga.

Our Legacy

Tennessee Sector 1 History

Tennessee Sector 1 represents the proud presence of Lambda Theta Phi across the Volunteer State. From our earliest chapters to our growing footprint at universities throughout the region, our brothers have upheld the founding ideals of scholarship, unity, and Latino culture.

1975

National Founding

Lambda Theta Phi founded at Kean University, Union, NJ — the first Latin fraternity in the United States.

Est.

Beta Eta Chapter — Vanderbilt

Lambda Theta Phi establishes its first Tennessee presence at Vanderbilt University in Nashville.

Est.

Gamma Gamma Chapter — UTK

Gamma Gamma chartered at UT Knoxville, bringing brotherhood to East Tennessee.

Est.

Epsilon Beta Chapter — UTC

Epsilon Beta brings Lambda Theta Phi to the Scenic City at the University of Tennessee at Chattanooga.

Recent

TAC1 — MTSU

Tennessee Associate Chapter 1 established at Middle Tennessee State University in Murfreesboro.

Today

Sector 1 United

Four chapters across Tennessee — united in brotherhood, scholarship, and service to the Latino community.
